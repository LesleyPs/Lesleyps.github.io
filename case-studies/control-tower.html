The Challenge

Tiki’s legacy B2B workflow suffered from operational silos, where product configuration and partner data were managed through disconnected manual tools. The objective was to consolidate these touchpoints into a single, cohesive command center.

Process Architecture

UX Evolution: Surfacing Utility

We mapped the onboarding architecture to move from a navigational model to a contextual one—bringing deeply buried product settings forward to eliminate friction.

V1: Hidden Settings

Users were forced to navigate to secondary screens to view or modify Frequency Caps.

V2: Contextual Control

Frequency data is surfaced on-card, with changes managed via a quick-modal trigger.

Strategic Outcomes

The implementation established a single source of truth for global product attributes, improving operational velocity and ecosystem integrity.

Operational Integrity

Eliminated data drift by centralizing configuration logic, ensuring high-fidelity visibility across regional platforms.

Scalable Framework

The modular component system allowed for rapid rollout of new product verticals with zero engineering redesign.
